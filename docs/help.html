ğŸ“–Position Types & Strategies

Short Put (Cash-Secured)

You're selling insurance. You agree to buy 100 shares at the strike price if the stock drops. You collect premium upfront.

  • Max Profit: Premium received
  • Max Loss: Strike Ã— 100 - Premium (if stock goes to $0)
  • Goal: Stock stays above strike, put expires worthless

Covered Call

You own shares and sell upside potential. If stock rises above strike, your shares get "called away" (sold).

  • Max Profit: (Strike - Cost Basis) Ã— 100 + Premium
  • Max Loss: Stock could drop to $0 (you still own shares)
  • Goal: Stock stays flat or rises slightly, call expires worthless

Bull Put Spread

Limited risk bullish bet. Sell a put AND buy a lower strike put for protection.

  • Max Profit: Net premium received
  • Max Loss: (Spread width - Premium) Ã— 100
  • Goal: Stock stays above higher strike

Call Debit Spread

Reduced-cost bullish bet. Buy a call AND sell a higher strike call to offset cost.

  • Max Profit: (Spread width - Premium paid) Ã— 100
  • Max Loss: Premium paid
  • Goal: Stock rises above higher strike

SKIPâ„¢ Call Strategy

LEAPS + shorter call combo. Buy a long-dated LEAPS call, then buy a shorter "SKIP" call at higher strike.

  • LEAPS: 12+ months out, acts as stock proxy
  • SKIP Call: 3-9 months out, higher strike, exit at 45-60 DTE
  • Goal: Capture gains on SKIP call, keep riding LEAPS

Buy/Write

Buy stock + immediately sell covered call. Also called "covered call at inception."
